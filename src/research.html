Yang, A., Zhang, G., & Andersen, E.  An exploration of annotated transposon sequences in C. elegans [poster presentation].  Quantitative Biology REU Symposium.

2022

Links: Poster, Repository 1, Repository 2

Overview: Transposable elements (TEs), or transposons, are mobile DNA segments that can move within the genome, influencing genomic variation and evolution. These elements belong to families with similar characteristics, contributing to phenotypic changes when they insert into coding or regulatory regions. To study natural variation in TEs across C. elegans strains, RNA-seq data is quantified by matching mRNA transcripts with a reference list of annotated transposon sequences. Existing online databases like WormBase and Dfam provide information on C. elegans TE DNA and mRNA sequences, aiding in this quantification process. The study aims to compare three lists of TE sequences, determine the best reference for quantification, and demonstrate methods for analyzing the resulting data.
